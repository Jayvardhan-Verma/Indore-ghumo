Religious places

Here are some famous religional places in indore which you should visit

Pitra Parvat

The one which is the major point of attraction here is 71 feet long and weighs 108 tons. Yes, this huge sculpture is made with 8 elements in golden color. It took 125 workers and 7 years to complete this huge beautiful deity of Lord Hanuman. It shines in bright golden color when the sun is in the sky and the same golden color looks utterly soothing and peaceful in the moonlight. Right below this huge sculpture has established a temple that has the small deity of Lord Hanuman for the devotees to worship. Lord Hanuman is seen sitting in the lap of Mother Anjani in this temple.

Location -

Pitra parvat, gandhinagar, Indore, Budhaniya, Madhya Pradesh 453112

Khajrana Temple

Citizens of Indore city and other nearby cities have great faith in the Khajrana temple. This temple was made by Ahilyabai Holkar, the brave Maratha queen. This is an important place of Hindus. Khajrana Ganesha Temple was constructed by Rani Ahilyabai Holkar. This temple is one of the famous Hindu temples in India. Mostly wednesday and sunday huge people will come to this temple for offering prayers. As per a local belief, all the wishes of the devotees are fulfilled, once they worship in this temple.

Location -

Ganeshpuri Main Road, Khajrana, Indore - 452016

Bijasan Temple

Bijasan is one of the temples of the Hindu Goddess Durga, situated in Indore District of Madhya Pradesh, India. Here a hillock (tekri) has a small temple of Bijasen Mata, built in 1920. Mela is held here during the Navaratri. This hill offers panoramic views of the sunset and of the city at night. Perched on top of the hill was a guest house of the Holkars, now converted into a Border Security Force Arms Museum. The surrounding area is being developed into gardens with a small lake. Ma Bijasan is Kuldevi of Hindu Gadariya(Kukroliya parivar).

Location -

Devi Ahillyabai Holkar Airport Area, Indore, Madhya Pradesh 453112

Annapurna Temple

Annapurna Temple is dedicated to the goddess Annapurna, the deity of food, and is one of the oldest pilgrimage sites in Indore. The temple was constructed in the 9th century and was rebuilt in 1959 by Mahamandaleshwar Swami Prabanandagirimharaj. The main gate of the temple was constructed in 1975. With a blend of Indo-Aryan and Dravidian architectural styles, the temple is regarded as the architectural heritage of the city due to its intricate carvings & detailed paintings.

Location -

Annapurna Rd, Annapurna Mandir, Kranti Kriplani Nagar, Indore, Madhya Pradesh 452009

Gomatgiri

Gomatgiri is situated amidst the natural environment of a hill in Indore. Gomatgiri is an important holy place for the Digambara sect of Jainism. The temple has a 21 feet statue of Gomateshwara which is a replica of the Bahubali statue of Shravanabegola. Another attractive feature of Gomatgiri is that it has 24 marble temples, each dedicated to 24 Tirthankaras of Jainism. The serene surroundings can act as a perfect place to sit and enjoy with a unique blend of peace and natural beauty.

Location -

Major District Rd, Nainod Village, Indore, Madhya Pradesh 257112
